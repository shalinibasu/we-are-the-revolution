July 18th

ON THIS DAY

On July 18th, 1753, Lemuel Haynes, the first Black person to be ordained as a minister in the United States, was born in West Hartford, Connecticut.

Lemuel Haynes was the son of a Scottish immigrant who was an indentured servant and an African American man who was enslaved. Haynes was an indentured servant for most of his childhood. He was also a churchgoer from a young age. At age 21, he was freed from servitude and enlisted in the Minutemen militia. Lemuel Haynes also later became the first African-American person in the United States to receive an honorary college degree.
